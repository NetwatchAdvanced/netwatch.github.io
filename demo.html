See what NetWatch actually looks like.

A walkthrough of the NetWatch dashboard on a live deployment: device activity, alert feed, geographic traffic, and DNS telemetry, all in a single operational view. Data shown is from a real network with all identifying information removed.

Alert Feed

Signature & Anomaly Alerts

Real-time feed of Suricata signature matches alongside heuristic anomaly flags; both detection layers in a single unified view, severity-ranked and timestamped.

Geographic Intelligence

Connection Mapping

Every external connection resolved to country, city, and threat classification. Connection volume, protocol breakdown, and per-IP investigation; all resolved locally, no external API.

DNS Telemetry

Per-Device Query History

90-day query log across every device on the network. Blocked domains, NXDOMAIN spikes, bypass attempts, and anomalous query volume, surfaced per device.

Device Inventory

Network Discovery

Every device that has appeared on the network: first seen, last seen, protocol activity, and alert history. Unknown devices flagged automatically.

Network Traffic

Traffic Overview

Real-time traffic volume across your network: bytes in and out per device, active connections, protocol distribution, and bandwidth utilization over time. Gives context to alert activity; a spike in outbound traffic at 2am means something different alongside a Suricata alert than it does alone.

Alert Trends

Alert History & Patterns

Alert activity over time rather than just the live feed. Recurring patterns from the same source, escalating severity trends, and quiet periods versus spikes. A single alert is an event; a week of the same alert firing nightly is a story worth investigating.
