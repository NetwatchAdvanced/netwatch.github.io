The Problem

Your network is a black box.

Enterprises have dedicated security teams, monitoring tools, and a helpdesk to call. Independent businesses have a router from the ISP and a hope that nothing goes wrong. That gap is real, the threats are not theoretical, and the absence of support options at this market segment is exactly the problem we are here to address.

No Visibility

You don't know what's on your network

POS terminals, staff devices, security cameras, smart TVs, and guest connections all share your infrastructure. Any of them can be compromised. Without visibility into what's connected and what it's doing, a device that's been quietly misbehaving for months looks identical to one that's fine.

Compliance Exposure

A flat network with a POS system is a PCI violation waiting to happen

PCI-DSS requires strict segmentation between payment processing systems and all other network traffic. Most independent business networks are completely flat, meaning your POS terminal and guest WiFi share the same infrastructure. That is not a security opinion, it is a compliance gap with real liability attached.

Silent Leakage

Your devices are talking without your permission

Telemetry, fingerprinting, and behavioral tracking happen constantly across network-connected devices. DNS bypass attempts, where devices try to ignore your resolver and phone home directly, are increasingly common. Your router doesn't log it and unless configured properly, it doesn't stop it either.

No Support Options

MSPs have client minimums that most independent businesses don't meet

Managed service providers are built around enterprise accounts. Their pricing, contracts, and minimum requirements consistently price out the independent business owner who needs help just as much. You've been left with consumer-grade gear and the assumption that your network doesn't need professional attention. It does.

No Segmentation

Every device on your network trusts every other

A typical business network is flat: POS terminals, staff laptops, security cameras, and guest WiFi all share the same infrastructure with no boundaries between them. If one device is compromised, everything else is reachable from it. Containment requires segmentation, which most business routers require deliberate configuration to achieve.

No Records or Logging

When something goes wrong, there's no timeline

Most businesses discover a network compromise from their bank, a payment processor, or a breach notification, not from their own network. By then the damage is done. Without logging there's no way to know when an issue started, what was accessed, or how long it had been happening. No record means no response.

Independent Business Network Security

The most common threats on independent business networks aren't sophisticated targeted attacks. They're passive and persistent: devices that were never properly segmented, DNS queries that were never filtered, traffic that was never inspected. The damage accumulates quietly over months, not in dramatic incidents. A single network-connected device sending telemetry data home hundreds of times per day does so entirely without your knowledge unless something is watching.

NetWatch Advanced is a detection system, not a prevention system. It does not block traffic, stop attacks, or intervene in connections. What it does is give you visibility you don't currently have: a continuous record of what's happening on your network, with alerting when something looks wrong.

That distinction matters. A smoke detector doesn't put out the fire, but you cannot respond to a fire you don't know is happening. Right now, most independent business networks have neither the detector nor anyone to call. NetWatch Advanced is both.

Why Not Just Buy a Security Product?

Consumer network security products do exist. Firewalla, Eero Secure, Netgear Armor are legitimate tools that provide a meaningful baseline for someone who wants something over nothing. But they are one-size-fits-all and place the entire burden of deployment and interpretation on you, the owner.

What they are, without exception, is products. You research them, buy them, install them yourself, interpret the alerts yourself, and when something looks wrong you spend hours trying to find an accurate answer. The detection they provide is largely signature-based: only known threats, known patterns, and known bad actors. If it's in the database, it gets flagged. If it isn't, it doesn't.

NetWatch Advanced deploys a two-layer detection model. Suricata's signature engine catches known threats against a continuously updated ruleset. The proprietary heuristic layer catches everything else, based on behavioral deviations from your network's established baseline. A device that suddenly triples its outbound connections at 2am is flagged not because it matches a known pattern, but because it doesn't match your pattern. That distinction is what makes it genuinely useful rather than just another alert feed to ignore.

Every deployment is preceded by a connectivity and coverage audit, configured specifically for your hardware and environment, and supported by direct access to the person who built and deployed the system. When you have a question, you're not opening a ticket with a support team in a timezone you've never been to. You're contacting the person who was in your building, knows your network, and has been watching it since day one.

That combination of heuristic and signature detection, purpose-built deployment, and a direct operator relationship doesn't exist as an off-the-shelf product. It exists as a service. That's what NetWatch Advanced is, and what makes it different.
